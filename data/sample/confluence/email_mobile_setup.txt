# Setting Up Corporate Email on iOS and Android

You can access your ACME Financial email, calendar, and contacts on a personal or corporate mobile device using Microsoft Outlook for Mobile. The native iOS Mail app and Android Gmail are not approved for corporate email — Outlook must be used.

## Prerequisites

- Your device must be enrolled in Intune (Microsoft's MDM) before email will activate. Enrollment takes approximately 5 minutes.
- Ensure you have completed MFA registration before starting.

## iOS Setup

### Step 1 — Enroll in Intune
1. Download the **Microsoft Intune Company Portal** app from the App Store.
2. Open it and sign in with your corporate email (firstname.lastname@acme-financial.com).
3. Follow the on-screen enrollment steps. You will be asked to install a management profile — tap Allow.
4. Return to the Company Portal and tap "Check Access" until your device shows as compliant (usually under 2 minutes).

### Step 2 — Set Up Outlook
1. Download **Microsoft Outlook** from the App Store if not already installed.
2. Open Outlook and tap "Add Account".
3. Enter your corporate email address and tap "Add Account".
4. Sign in with your corporate credentials and complete the MFA prompt.
5. Outlook will configure your mailbox, calendar, and contacts automatically.

## Android Setup

### Step 1 — Enroll in Intune
1. Download the **Microsoft Intune Company Portal** app from the Play Store.
2. Open it and sign in with your corporate email.
3. Tap "Enroll Device" and follow the prompts. Accept the device management profile when prompted.
4. Wait for the compliance check to complete (green checkmark in Company Portal).

### Step 2 — Set Up Outlook
1. Download **Microsoft Outlook** from the Play Store.
2. Open Outlook → Add Account → enter your corporate email.
3. Sign in and complete MFA.
4. Your mailbox will sync automatically.

## PIN and Encryption Requirements

By policy, any device with corporate email installed must have a device PIN or biometric lock enabled with a screen timeout of no more than 5 minutes. Intune will enforce this — if your device is non-compliant, email access will be blocked until a PIN is set.

## Remote Wipe

If your device is lost or stolen, IT can remotely wipe **only the Outlook app and its data** — not your personal photos or apps. Report a lost device immediately to the helpdesk at ext. 4357 so this can be performed.

## Troubleshooting

**"Your device is not compliant" message:** Open the Intune Company Portal and tap "Check Access". If the issue persists, re-enroll the device.

**MFA prompt loops repeatedly:** Your Authenticator session has expired. Re-authenticate in the Authenticator app, then retry Outlook sign-in.

**Emails not syncing:** Check your mobile data or Wi-Fi connection. In Outlook settings, verify the sync period is not set to "None".

**Outlook app crashes on launch:** Force-close the app, clear the cache (Android: Settings → Apps → Outlook → Clear Cache), and relaunch. If the issue continues, uninstall and reinstall Outlook.
