# Microsoft Teams — Common Issues and Fixes

## Teams Not Loading or Stuck on the Loading Screen

1. Quit Teams completely (right-click the Teams icon in the system tray → Quit, or Force Quit on macOS).
2. Clear the Teams cache:
   - **Windows:** Press Win+R → type `%appdata%\Microsoft\Teams` → delete the contents of the `Cache` folder.
   - **macOS:** Open Finder → Go → Go to Folder → `~/Library/Application Support/Microsoft/Teams` → delete the `Cache` folder.
3. Relaunch Teams. The cache rebuilds on next sign-in (takes 30–60 seconds).

## Messages Not Sending or Showing as "Pending"

This usually indicates a network or authentication issue:
1. Check your internet connection.
2. Sign out of Teams (profile picture → Sign Out) and sign back in.
3. If on VPN, try disconnecting and reconnecting.
4. If the issue persists, restart Teams.

## Teams Calls — No Audio or Video

**Before a call:**
- Check that the correct microphone, speaker, and camera are selected: Teams settings → Devices.
- Test your devices using the "Make a test call" button in Teams settings.

**Microphone not working:**
- Windows: Settings → Privacy → Microphone → ensure Teams is allowed.
- macOS: System Preferences → Security & Privacy → Privacy → Microphone → check Microsoft Teams.

**Camera not working:**
- Check that no other application (Zoom, browser) is using the camera at the same time.
- macOS: System Preferences → Security & Privacy → Privacy → Camera → check Microsoft Teams.
- Try unplugging and replugging a USB camera.

**Echo during calls:** If others hear an echo of their own voice, your speakers are feeding back into your microphone. Use headphones or enable noise suppression: Teams settings → Devices → Noise suppression → High.

## Teams Notifications Not Working

- Check Teams notification settings: profile picture → Settings → Notifications.
- Windows: Settings → System → Notifications → ensure Teams notifications are on.
- macOS: System Preferences → Notifications → Teams → enable Allow Notifications.
- If notifications work in the browser but not the desktop app, reinstall Teams.

## "You're missing out — ask your admin to enable Microsoft Teams"

Your account is licensed and enabled. This message appears when Teams is not yet provisioned for your account. Contact the helpdesk — provisioning usually completes within 1 business day of your start date.

## Teams Very Slow or Using Too Much CPU/Memory

Teams can be memory-intensive. Workarounds:
1. Disable GPU hardware acceleration: Teams settings → General → uncheck "Disable GPU hardware acceleration" and restart Teams.
2. Reduce the number of open chats/channels.
3. Use Teams in a browser (teams.microsoft.com) as a lighter alternative.

## Re-installing Teams

If none of the above resolves the issue, a clean reinstall usually fixes persistent problems:
1. Uninstall Teams via Control Panel (Windows) or drag to Trash (macOS).
2. Delete the cache folder (paths above).
3. Download the latest Teams installer from https://teams.microsoft.com/downloads.
4. Install and sign in with your corporate credentials.
