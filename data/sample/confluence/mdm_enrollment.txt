# Enrolling a New Device in Microsoft Intune (MDM)

All company-issued devices must be enrolled in Microsoft Intune (our Mobile Device Management platform) before they can access corporate resources such as email, SharePoint, or internal applications.

## Windows Laptop Enrollment

New laptops provisioned by IT are enrolled automatically during setup via Autopilot. If your device is not enrolled:

1. Open Settings → Accounts → Access work or school.
2. Click "Connect" → enter your corporate email (firstname.lastname@acme-financial.com).
3. On the sign-in screen, enter your AD credentials.
4. Select "Join this device to Azure Active Directory" when prompted.
5. Restart the device. Intune policies will apply during the restart.

Enrollment may take 10–15 minutes. The Company Portal app will appear in the Start menu when enrollment is complete.

## macOS Enrollment

1. Download the Company Portal app from the Mac App Store or from https://itportal.acme-financial.internal/downloads.
2. Open Company Portal → Sign in with your corporate email.
3. Follow the guided enrollment steps. You will be prompted to install a management profile.
4. Go to System Preferences → Profiles → click the Intune profile → Install.
5. Enter your macOS admin password when prompted (contact the helpdesk if you do not have local admin rights).
6. Enrollment completes in 5–10 minutes.

## iPhone / iPad Enrollment

1. Open Settings → General → VPN & Device Management.
2. If a company profile is already listed, it was enrolled by IT. No action needed.
3. For a new device: download the Microsoft Intune Company Portal from the App Store.
4. Sign in with your corporate email and complete the enrollment wizard.
5. You will be prompted to install a management profile — this is required and is limited to corporate policy enforcement; IT cannot access personal data, photos, or apps.

## Android Enrollment

1. Download Microsoft Intune Company Portal from Google Play.
2. Open the app → Sign in with corporate email.
3. Follow the enrollment steps. Allow the required device permissions when prompted.
4. For Samsung Knox devices, additional MDM features are enabled automatically.

## What MDM Manages on Your Device

Intune manages corporate data only. It can:
- Enforce screen lock PIN/password requirements
- Require device encryption
- Push approved apps via Software Center/Self Service
- Remotely wipe corporate data if the device is lost or stolen (does NOT wipe personal data on BYOD)

IT cannot access personal files, photos, browser history, or messages through Intune.

## Unenrolling / Device Retirement

If you are leaving the company or replacing your device, contact the IT Helpdesk before doing anything. They will remote-wipe corporate data and unenroll the device properly. Do not factory reset a company-issued device without contacting IT first.
